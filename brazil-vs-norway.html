⚔️ Tactical Deep Dive

🇧🇷 Brazil

In Possession: Bruno G. drops between CBs to build; full-backs push high. Vinícius drifts central; Raphinha operates as a free #10. Rodrygo makes late runs from the right.

Out of Possession: 4-4-2 press. Gabriel's recovery pace is elite. Key question: can João Gomes cover behind Danilo against Nusa's pace?

Set Pieces: Gabriel (6'3") + Marquinhos are genuine aerial threats. Scored vs Japan from a Raphinha corner.

🇳🇴 Norway

In Possession: Win it → Ødegaard between lines → Haaland channel run within 3-4 passes. Nusa's dribbling (4.2/game) is the secondary outlet.

Out of Possession: Compact 4-5-1 mid-block. Concede wings, clog middle. Force crosses; Ajer (6'5") clears. Midfield three deny Raphinha the half-turn.

Set Pieces: Haaland + Ajer = tallest attacking pair. Norway's 3 set-piece goals lead all R16 teams.

🎯 Critical Matchups

⚡ Gabriel vs Haaland

The defining duel. Gabriel has the speed to stay with Haaland, but Haaland's movement between CB and FB is almost unmarkable. If isolated 1v1, Brazil are in trouble.

🧠 Bruno G. vs Ødegaard

Two elite PL midfielders. If Ødegaard receives facing forward, Haaland's run is triggered. Whoever wins this chess match controls the game.

🔥 Vinícius vs Ryerson

Ryerson has never faced a ball-carrier at this level in a knockout game. Expect Berge to double-team on that side.

🛡️ Alisson vs 1v1

World's best 1v1 keeper vs the most clinical finisher. Alisson stopped 3 big chances vs Japan; he'll need that again.
